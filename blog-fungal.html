Nail Fungal Infection

Step into Renewal, Say Goodbye to Fungal Intrusion

What to expect

Throughout the procedure, the nurse ensures your comfort and safety, addressing any concerns or questions you may have. They prioritize maintaining a sterile environment and following proper infection control protocols to minimize the risk of complications.

The process of removing nail fungal Infection.

Assessment: A healthcare professional examines the affected nails and evaluates the severity of the fungal infection. They may inquire about your symptoms and medical history to ensure the most appropriate treatment.

Preparation: Before starting the treatment, the healthcare professional may clean the nails and surrounding skin to remove any debris or nail polish. This allows for better penetration of the antifungal medication.

Topical Antifungal Treatment: The healthcare professional may prescribe or recommend an over-the-counter antifungal cream, ointment, or nail lacquer. These medications are applied directly to the affected nails and surrounding skin. It's important to follow the instructions provided and continue the treatment for the prescribed duration.

Nail Debridement: In some cases, the healthcare professional may trim, file, or remove the infected parts of the nail. This helps reduce the fungal load and improves the effectiveness of topical or oral antifungal treatments.

Preventive Measures: To prevent reinfection and promote healing, it is important to maintain good foot hygiene. This includes keeping the feet clean and dry, wearing breathable socks and shoes, avoiding sharing personal items, and disinfecting nail clippers and other tools.

Follow-up and Monitoring: It is common to have follow-up appointments to monitor the progress of the treatment and make any necessary adjustments. If the infection persists or worsens, further evaluation or alternative treatment options may be considered.
